WHY A BLOG?

17th March 2026

In the retrospect of having now built this site, as diminutive as it may be, I feel its necessary to explain why I think its a good idea.

For the most part, I have not been an active participant within the communities of the technologies and practises I am involved in. I lurk on notable blogs, RFC's, and threads as a passive observer with no notable presence. If I were asked to recount examples where I contributed in these spaces I would fail to recall any. And there is nothing inherently wrong about that! Yet it bothers me.

When I first discovered programming I was very turned off by the broad all-encompassing tutorials I found. Learning the ins and outs of a specific language didn't interested me, and the guidance I found instructing how things needed to be done was irritating even if technically sound. That early on, all I wanted was a puzzle to play with. The experience I was looking for was that which fostered an innate curiosity and freedom. I broke things a lot, mostly intentionally. My code was first copied off Stack Overflow or a random GitHub repository, then ripped apart and reassembled over and over again. I learnt through play, by leaning into my curiosity.

That may be a little confusing, the point is this: I didn't want to learn to program by reading a manual, I instead wanted to learn by playing with pre-existing parts. By virtue of this preference, I was an inexperienced kid with a keyboard sitting in the same channels industry experts. I was too clueless to understand what questions I should have been asking and so I never asked any. But I'm not in that same position now.

There are still a substantial number of conversations in these communities that I feel unworthy to raise my voice in. But there are also those in which I think I should. I'm experienced enough now to notice oversights, to suggest improvements, and to comment on the technologies and practises we engage with. I want to share my thoughts, ideas, and opinions with these communities. The act of creating this site serves as a loose declaration of my desire to do so. By founding this space where I speak publicly, it acts as an affirmation to myself that I will be more openly involved in these communities.

I am well aware of the reality that next to no-one will ever visit this site aside from my future self. But, the fact that anyone theoretically could find it is meaning enough.
